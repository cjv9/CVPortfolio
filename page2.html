How the Internet Works

Project Description

Made with the intention of informing of the protocols and basic operations that run the Internet, 'How the Internet Works' is a website designed and created completely by myself. This assignment had to be hosted on Github, have a working navigation bar, and show sufficient knowledge of working with Github.

Creative Process

For this website, the idea was to make an attractive site that explained essential protocols that run the Internet. I focused on explaining domain names, ICANN IP addresses, HTML, CSS, and Routing. I chose a shade of blue as the sites background, which complimented the light grey and white colors of the navigation bars, title cards, and sidebars. There is a call to action and a link to all the pages in a homepage, and a dropdown navigation bar, as well as a side navigation bar are provided to make finding pages easier.

Obstacles and Reflection

As my first solo website, I was in charge of both the design and content. I found it difficult to align the text, side cards, and top navigation bar, and had to learn to keep all my CSS consistent throughout my pages. I had a hard time figuring out how to host my site on Github, which was eventually relieved by doing some research of my course materials. After getting through the first two pages, the rest were easier to do, and the product is a working site that is highly informative. Click here to view 'How the Internet Works'
