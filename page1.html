History of the Internet

Project Description

'History of the Internet' was a team assignment, of two members, meant to inform about how the Internet was created. Around six different pages were meant to be creates, all having to do with one aspect of the creation of the Internet, how big browser sites were created, and major controversies throughout the initial years of the Internet. Bootstrap was meant to be primarily used, and was supposed to be hosted on either AFS, or Github. This specific website was meant to be split between content and design, and my role was content creation.

Creative Process

For this site, although I wasn't in charge of the design, I went about assisting my partner in the creation process by establishing what elements would make the site easy to navigate. The dropdown navbar was essential for me in order to make it as user friendly as possible, and the image side card was also an element I contributed, with the idea of making the site eye catching. As for content, after viewing enough documentaries and doing enough research on each topic, I decided on writing up the most interesting, and important parts of the material. HTML was used to create well proportioned paragraphs.

Obstacles, and Reflections

As my first project, there was a serious hindrance of limited knowledge of CSS, HTML, and the use of AFS and Github. My partner and I had a hard time with Github collaboration, which we eventually figured out by testing different combinations of settings on the repository of the site. Although it was daunting at first to make a website from scratch for the first time, good communication between my partner and I, and a good amount of looking into different ways of creating cards, sidebars, favicons, different HTML codes, we made a working website. This site is both strong in content, and design, all links work correctly, and is hosted both on AFS and Github. Overall, it was a great learning experience, and a good introduction to developing websites, and working collaboratively.
